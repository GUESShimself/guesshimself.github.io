Selected Work

CAP Design System

Conduent

2020-2024

Multi-brand, tokenized design system built from 0-to-1 to support Conduent's automation platform and product suite.

  • 3+ product teams
  • Web platform
  • Token architecture, component library, adoption strategy

System-Level Decisions

  • Designed a semantic token layer to enable multi-brand theming without duplicating components or fragmenting the system.
  • Optimized for API stability over feature velocity—breaking changes required explicit deprecation and migration paths.
  • Invested in adoption tooling (linting, migration scripts) before documentation to reduce friction for product teams.
  • Kept the system opinionated at the token level while allowing flexibility at the component layer.

Governance & Adoption

Established a lightweight governance model centered on regular cross-team reviews and clear ownership. Adoption scaled from a single team to 3+ product teams within six months, driven primarily by tooling, support, and trust rather than mandate.

Design intent becomes durable when it's encoded as tokens and APIs — not screens.

Conduent Automation Platform

Conduent

2020-2024

Low-code workflow automation platform enabling teams to design, configure, and deploy enterprise solutions faster.

  • Global, distributed teams
  • Web platform
  • Workflow builder, dashboards, user management

Key Challenges

  • Designing interfaces that scale from simple workflows to highly complex automation without overwhelming users
  • Balancing power for engineers with approachability for less technical operators through progressive disclosure and clear patterns
  • Aligning design intent across globally distributed teams with consistent documentation and shared terminology

What I Delivered

  • Workflow builder interaction model: node types, configuration panels, validation, and error handling
  • Dashboard system: chart-selection guidelines, color rules, standardized layouts, and configurable widgets
  • User management UX: simplified role/permission flows with scalable search and filtering

Core Constraint

The same platform had to support rapid internal prototyping while also acting as a production-grade surface for enterprise deployment—so usability improvements could never compromise auditability, permissions, or clarity under risk.

Impact

  • ~40% faster solution deployment for client implementations
  • ~40% reduction in UI development time through component reuse
  • Faster onboarding through standardized patterns and documentation templates

Mobile Facilities Management

FM:Systems

2019-2020

Redesign of a legacy facilities management app for maintenance technicians, delivering 60% faster task completion and full offline capability.

  • Solo designer, end-to-end
  • iOS/Android
  • Field research, offline-first UX

Design Constraints

  • Unreliable connectivity in mechanical rooms and multi-building campuses—offline-first was a hard requirement.
  • Time-critical work (including emergencies) demanded fast capture, clear status, and low-error interaction patterns.

What I Focused On

  • Streamlined work-order flows to reduce steps, minimize typing, and keep primary actions obvious under pressure.
  • Offline UX: clear sync states, resilient drafts, and predictable recovery when connectivity returns.

Outcome

Usability testing showed a 60% reduction in task completion time. The redesign delivered 100% offline capability and improved response speed and data quality for maintenance teams.
